Biography

Born on 28 March 1995 in Tunis, Tunisia, Amen Allah Lamrani grew up between Tunisian and Moroccan artistic heritage, influenced by his mother Thouraya Bousrih, a French teacher, and his father, Moulay Cherif Lamrani, founder of the iconic band Lemchaheb. This environment sparked his passion for sound and led him to pursue university studies in the field, earning both a bachelor's and a master's degree in sound.

Today, he is an Audio Engineer and Sound Designer specializing in cinematic sound. He blends technical precision with emotional storytelling to craft immersive soundscapes through sound editing, Foley, scoring, and mixing, consistently elevating the sonic dimension of film and audiovisual projects.
